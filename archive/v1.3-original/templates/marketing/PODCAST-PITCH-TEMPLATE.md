# Podcast Pitch Template

Use this template to pitch your WordPress plugin story to podcast hosts. Personalize heavily for each podcast.

---

## Research Checklist (Complete Before Pitching)

Before sending your pitch, research the podcast:

- [ ] Listen to 3-5 recent episodes
- [ ] Identify episode format (interview, panel, solo, etc.)
- [ ] Note typical episode length
- [ ] Understand their audience
- [ ] Check if they've featured similar guests
- [ ] Find host's contact email (preferred) or use contact form
- [ ] Look at host's social media for recent interests/topics
- [ ] Check if they have guest guidelines or submission forms

## Pre-Interview Preparation

Once your pitch is accepted:

### Before the Recording

- [ ] Confirm date, time, and time zone
- [ ] Clarify recording format (video, audio only)
- [ ] Ask about episode length and format
- [ ] Request any specific topics they want to cover
- [ ] Send your one-pager/media kit
- [ ] Provide 8-10 sample questions
- [ ] Test your tech setup (mic, camera, internet)
- [ ] Prepare 2-3 key stories to tell
- [ ] Have talking points ready (but stay conversational)
- [ ] Prepare questions to ask the host (if format allows)

### Day of Recording

- [ ] Quiet, well-lit space
- [ ] Test equipment 15 minutes early
- [ ] Water nearby
- [ ] Phone on silent
- [ ] Browser tabs closed (avoid notifications)
- [ ] Notes nearby but not reading from them
- [ ] Smile (even on audio-only)
- [ ] Energy level high
- [ ] Have fun!

## Tips for Success

1. **Research is non-negotiable:** Personalize every pitch
2. **Lead with value:** What can you teach their audience?
3. **Be specific:** Vague pitches get ignored
4. **Show, don't tell:** Use specific results and stories
5. **Make it easy:** Provide everything they need
6. **Follow up once:** But only once
7. **Respect their time:** Keep pitches concise
8. **Be genuine:** Authentic enthusiasm shows
9. **Promote heavily:** Help the podcast, help yourself
10. **Build relationships:** Think long-term, not transactional
